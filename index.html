Three decisions that fail procurement review.

Most tablet procurement fails on zone, standard, or gas-group mismatch.

Zone 1 vs Zone 2.

Zone 1 = explosive atmosphere likely during normal ops. Zone 2 = only under abnormal conditions. A Zone 1 tablet covers both zones. A Zone 2 tablet cannot enter Zone 1 areas. Most process-area deployments require Zone 1 certification.

ATEX vs IECEx.

ATEX is mandatory in the EU/EEA. IECEx is the international scheme accepted in 50+ countries. For global deployments across multiple regions, choose dual-certified tablets. Many vendors certify under both frameworks.

IIA, IIB, IIC.

Gas groups classify ignition risk. IIC (hydrogen) is the hardest to certify for and covers IIA + IIB. If your site handles hydrogen or acetylene, the tablet must be IIC rated. Most modern Ex tablets target IIC for maximum site flexibility.

Field tablet use cases, honestly.

Tablets in hazardous areas serve different roles than phones. Larger screens enable workflows that phones can't.

Inspection & reporting.

Primary use case

  • Form-based inspection checklists on 8–10″ screens
  • Photo documentation with annotation
  • PDF/drawing review in the field
  • Barcode/QR scanning for asset tracking

Process & operations.

Growing use case

  • SCADA/HMI dashboards at the process unit
  • Digital work orders and permit-to-work
  • Video calls for remote expert support
  • ERP/SAP access in hazardous zones
