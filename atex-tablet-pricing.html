Understanding the Price Tiers

€2,000–€3,000 · Entry Zone 1

The i.safe MOBILE IS930.1 sits alone in this tier — the most affordable Zone 1 tablet on the market. The trade-off is a smaller 8-inch display and slightly older Android version. For organisations that need Zone 1 certification on a budget, or where the compact form factor is actually preferred, this is the clear choice.

€3,000–€4,000 · Mid-Range

Most tablets cluster here. The ecom Tab-Ex 03 DZ2 (€3,200) offers the best balance of screen size, Samsung ecosystem, and Zone 1 certification. The Tab-Ex Pro DZ2 (€3,800) adds an AMOLED display and S-Pen for an extra €600. The Zone 2-only Zebra XPAD L10 and Getac F110-Ex also fall here, offering larger screens or Windows OS at the cost of Zone 2 limitation.

€4,000+ · Premium / Windows Zone 1

The Aegex 10 IS at €4,500 is the only Zone 1 Windows tablet. The premium reflects the engineering complexity of achieving intrinsic safety certification with a full Windows OS and the niche market. Only justified when legacy Windows enterprise applications (SAP GUI, custom SCADA clients) must run natively in Zone 1.

Key Takeaways

  • Zone 1 costs more than Zone 2 — intrinsic safety certification for Zone 1 adds engineering and testing costs. Expect a 20–40% premium over Zone 2 equivalents.
  • Windows costs more than Android — the Aegex 10 IS is the most expensive tablet primarily because Windows Zone 1 certification is rare and complex.
  • Don't over-specify — if your hazardous area is Zone 2, don't pay for Zone 1 certification. The Getac F110-Ex and Zebra XPAD L10 offer large screens at Zone 2 prices.
  • Request quotes — list prices are negotiable, especially for fleet orders of 20+ units. Most distributors offer volume discounts of 10–15%.
